Lecture-15 - Stratification and Health

Evictions

Matt Desmond is a Sociologist at Princeton University. We brougt him up in Lecture-14 in reference to his research on wildland fire fighters. His work on inequality in the United States has resulted in a book on evictions as well as a national project aimed at tracking the eviction rate in the Unisted States

Poverty Line

The federal government’s poverty line is used to determine whether or not individuals or their families are eligible for a range of social saftey-net services. It varies based on family size, and different dollar amounts are used for Hawaii and Alaska. Mollie Orshansky (1915-2006) who established the system used for calculating these poverty line values in the 1960s when she was employed as a statistician at the Social Security Administration. She used a basic assumption that a third of a family’s budget would be spent on food. The methods she used have been continually critiqued, but remain very influential for determining access to SNAP, TANF, Medicaid, and WIC, among other programs.
