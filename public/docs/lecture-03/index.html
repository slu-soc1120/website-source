Lecture-03 - Fundamental Causes and the Social Determinants of Health

Middle Range Theory

Unlike “grand” theories that start with a theoretical idea and attempt to explain a large number of phenomena, “mid-” or “middle-range theory” attempts to address a concrete and relatively narrow empirical issue. The idea was put forth by Robert K. Merton, Ph.D. (1910-2003), who spent the majority of his career at Columbia University. The original idea was discussed in a 1949 paper called “On Sociological Theories of the Middle Range.".

Model for the Social Determiants of Health

The model presented in class was originally published by the National Academy of Sciences in a report called “Promoting Health: Intervention Strategies from Social and Behavioral Research.” I call it the “Kaplan model” because it was featured in a 2004 article by a public health professor at the University of Michigan named George Kaplan. That article, titled “What’s Wrong with Social Epidemiology, and How Can We Make It Better?," which discusses the both the strengths and the weakensses of these types of models.
