Lecture-12 - Deviance

People

We briefly mentioned the following people:

  • Émile Durkheim (1858-1917) - French sociologist known for his work on mental health, religion, and the division of labor. In particular, we discussed his ideas suicidie. Though our perspectives on this have changed, a Durkheimian approach to sucidie remains in sociology.
  • Harold Garfinkel (1917-2011) - American sociologist who established the field of ethnomethodology. He was also an early practioner of breaching experiments for studying social order and deviance.
  • Howard Becker (b. 1928) - American sociologist
  • Robert Merton (1910-2003) - American sociologist who is credited with the strain theory approach to understanding deviance.
